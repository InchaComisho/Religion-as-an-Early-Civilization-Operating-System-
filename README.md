## Overview

**Religion as an Early Civilization Operating System** is a civilizational essay that interprets religious and mythological systems not merely as belief systems or sources of conflict, but as early **civilization operating systems** created to help human societies survive before the development of scientific frameworks.

This document does not argue against religion itself.

Rather, it proposes that religions and myths were originally designed to reduce conflict, maintain community order, preserve relationships with nature, and transmit long-term survival strategies.

In this framework, religion served as an early civilization OS with the following functions:

- teaching ethical norms
- maintaining social order
- explaining the relationship between humanity and nature
- suppressing conflict
- transmitting behavioral guidance for survival
- expressing invisible causal structures through symbols

However, this original non-dual wisdom was later distorted by dualistic civilizations.

The central thesis is:

> Religion did not create human conflict.  
> Civilizations that interpreted religion dualistically transformed religion into a tool of conflict.

Major religions and mythological systems across the world share structural patterns that transcend cultural boundaries.

These include:

- loss of paradise
- emergence of good and evil
- human arrogance
- apocalyptic collapse
- deviation from natural order
- restoration of harmony
- renewal of the world

These are not merely stories.

They may be symbolic systems created before scientific language existed, designed to transmit warnings about civilizational danger, natural law, social collapse, ethics, and community survival.

This document reinterprets religion and mythology as follows:

```text
Religion and mythology
= pre-scientific civilization OS
= symbolic manuals for ethics, order, natural understanding, and community stability
```

The root of human conflict is not religion itself, but the reinterpretation of religion through dualistic categories such as good versus evil, saved versus damned, and us versus them.

## 1. Introduction: Is Religion Truly the Cause of Conflict?

In modern discussions, religion is often framed as a cause of conflict.

Religious wars, sectarian disputes, heresy persecutions, civilizational clashes, and doctrinal conflicts have certainly existed throughout history.

However, structurally speaking, the problem may not be religion itself, but how religion was interpreted.

Many religions and myths, despite differences in expression, share common goals:

- warning against human arrogance
- restraining runaway desire
- preserving community
- encouraging a return to natural order
- placing humanity within nature and the cosmos
- warning of civilizational collapse through end-times narratives

From this perspective, religion can be interpreted not as a system created to produce conflict, but as an early civilization OS designed to prevent conflict.

## 2. Religion as a Pre-Scientific Operating System

Before modern science, statistics, psychology, sociology, ecology, and systems theory existed, human societies needed another form of knowledge preservation.

Religion and mythology fulfilled that role.

### Functions of Religion

- ethical norms
- social cohesion
- reverence for ecosystems
- conflict avoidance
- long-term survival strategies
- warnings against power abuse
- regulation of desire
- meaning-making around death and uncertainty

Religious narratives were not merely literal histories.  
They were symbolic instruction manuals for preventing the collapse of communities.

In this sense, religion was humanity’s earliest attempt at:

- systems design
- social engineering
- behavioral regulation
- ecological ethics
- preservation of long-term memory
- construction of community operating systems

## 3. The Forbidden Fruit: The Birth of Dualism

The story of Adam and Eve is often read as a story of disobedience or moral failure.

Structurally, however, it can be read as the moment when humanity acquired dualistic cognition.

### What the Forbidden Fruit Symbolizes

- good / evil
- right / wrong
- self / other
- God / humanity
- nature / humanity
- domination / submission

When dualism appears, the world is no longer perceived as a field of relationships, but as a field of separation and opposition.

In this sense, “expulsion from Eden” is not punishment.  
It is the natural consequence of adopting a perception that divides reality.

From the world of harmony to the world of separation:  
this is the beginning of dualism.

## 6. Why Dualistic Civilizations Misread Religion

If religions and myths contained non-dual wisdom, why did so many civilizations turn them into tools of conflict?

The answer is structural.

---

### 6.1 Dualistic Cultures Cannot Read Non-Dual Texts

A dualistic worldview converts everything into opposition.

- God / Satan
- believer / unbeliever
- saved / damned
- justice / enemy
- inside / outside

Under such cognition, the original non-dual message becomes unreadable.

Even stories that teach harmony can be read as stories about defeating enemies.

---

### 6.2 Power Structures Depend on Dualism

Non-dualism weakens rigid power structures.

This is because non-dualism relativizes:

- hierarchy
- authority
- clerical power
- political control
- economic privilege
- governance that requires enemies

For power structures, it is often more convenient for religion to be interpreted dualistically than non-dualistically.

---

### 6.3 How Religion Becomes a Tool of Conflict

The transformation can be summarized as follows:

```text
non-dual symbolic system
→ dualistic interpretation
→ fixed good and evil
→ creation of enemies
→ use by power structures
→ religious conflict
```

Therefore, the problem is not religion itself.

The problem is that dualistic interpretation corrupted religion.

Under this framework, religion is not the cause of human conflict.

Religion was originally a harmonizing OS designed to teach:

- non-duality
- coexistence
- ecological balance
- social harmony
- regulation of desire
- reverence for natural law
- warnings about civilizational collapse

The true “original sin” is not disobedience.  
It is **dualistic perception**.

Once dualism enters the center of civilization, society moves toward structures of good and evil, allies and enemies, domination and submission. As encoded in end-times myths across the world, such civilizations eventually move toward collapse.

Religion, myth, philosophy, and civilizational dynamics can be understood as one unified model.

Religion originally existed not for conflict, but to prevent civilizational collapse.

The next path for humanity is not to read religion as a weapon of division.  
It is to recover the non-dual wisdom, natural law, and principle of Wa hidden beneath its symbolic structures.
